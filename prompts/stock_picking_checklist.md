# Stock-Picking Checklist: Quality-at-Value Framework

## Before You Buy Any Stock, Answer These Questions

### 1. QUALITY METRICS (Pass/Fail Criteria)
Rate each on a scale: ✓ (Pass), ≈ (Marginal), ✗ (Fail)

#### Profitability & Returns
- [ ] **ROIC (Return on Invested Capital) >15%?**
  - Where to find: Screener or annual report → Look for "ROIC" or calculate: NOPAT / (Total Assets - Current Liabilities)
  - Why: Shows how efficiently the company uses capital. >15% = sustainable competitive advantage.

- [ ] **Debt/Equity Ratio <0.5x?**
  - Where to find: Balance sheet → Total Debt / Total Equity
  - Why: Lower debt = safer during downturns + lower financial risk.

- [ ] **Net Profit Margin ≥15% and stable for 3+ years?**
  - Where to find: Income statement → Net Income / Revenue
  - Why: Profitable, predictable business. Margins shouldn't wobble wildly.

#### Cash Generation
- [ ] **Free Cash Flow (FCF) positive and growing?**
  - Where to find: Cash flow statement → Operating CF - Capital Expenditure
  - Why: FCF is real cash. Companies that don't generate it will eventually fail.

- [ ] **FCF Conversion: (FCF / Net Profit) ≥70%?**
  - Where to find: FCF / Net Income
  - Why: Earnings quality check. If company reports ₹100Cr profit but FCF is ₹20Cr, earnings are low-quality.

#### Growth Track Record
- [ ] **Revenue CAGR last 3 years ≥12%?**
  - Where to find: Income statement, multiple years
  - Why: Consistent growth signals a healthy business model.

- [ ] **No surprise losses, provisions, or one-off charges in last 3 years?**
  - Where to find: Annual reports → Notes to accounts
  - Why: One-offs are red flags. Too many = management hiding issues.

**DECISION: If you mark 6+ as "✓" and none as "✗", proceed to Valuation. If 2+ are "✗", skip this stock.**

### 2. VALUATION (Your Entry Point)
**Rule: Only buy if stock is 20-30% cheaper than its fair value.**

#### Valuation Metrics
Choose ONE of these as your primary trigger:

- [ ] **Price-to-Earnings (PE) Ratio:**
  - Calculate: Stock Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)
  - Your trigger: Buy if current PE is 20-30% below 5-year average PE
  - Example: If average PE is 25x, buy when PE drops to 17.5-20x
  - Where to check: Screener (moneycontrol, tickertape) or financial site

- [ ] **Price-to-Book (PB) Ratio:**
  - Calculate: Market Cap / Book Value of Equity
  - Your trigger: Buy if PB <2.0x (especially for banks/NBFCs, target <1.5x)
  - Example: HDFC Bank trading at 2.8x PB = wait; at 1.8x PB = consider

- [ ] **Dividend Yield:**
  - Calculate: Annual Dividend Per Share / Stock Price × 100
  - Your trigger: Buy if yield is >2% AND company has stable 10+ year dividend history
  - Example: HDFC Bank at 2.5% dividend yield + 25-year dividend track record = strong signal

- [ ] **EV/EBITDA Multiple:**
  - For advanced users: Enterprise Value / EBITDA
  - Your trigger: Buy if EV/EBITDA is 15-20% below historical average
  - Example: Telecom stocks: if 10-year avg EV/EBITDA is 6.5x, buy at 5.2x or less

#### Margin of Safety
- [ ] **Downside protection: If stock drops 30% from entry, is the thesis still intact?**
  - Example: You buy TCS at ₹3,500. If it drops to ₹2,450, would you still own it? If YES, you have margin of safety.
  - If NO, don't buy at ₹3,500.

**DECISION: Pick ONE valuation metric above. If stock meets that trigger, proceed. If not, wait.**

### 3. BUSINESS MOAT (Competitive Advantage)
**Question: Why will this company still be successful in 5 years?**

Rate each moat type (check all that apply):

#### Brand & Pricing Power
- [ ] **Recognized brand that customers prefer over competitors?**
  - Examples: Nestlé, Bajaj (motorcycles), Britannia
  - Why: Can raise prices without losing customers = sustainable profit margins.

#### Network Effects / Scale
- [ ] **Stronger as it grows? (More users = more value for everyone)**
  - Examples: HDFC Bank (largest network), NSE (exchange monopoly)
  - Why: Competitors struggle to catch up.

#### Cost Advantage
- [ ] **Lowest-cost producer in the industry?**
  - Examples: Reliance (integrated supply chain), some telecom players
  - Why: Can undercut competition while still being profitable.

#### Switching Costs
- [ ] **Customers have high switching costs?**
  - Examples: Banks (changing account = friction), software licenses
  - Why: Customers stick around even if competition appears.

#### Intangible Assets
- [ ] **Patents, licenses, proprietary tech that competitors don't have?**
  - Examples: Pharma companies, software products
  - Why: Defensible market position for years.

**DECISION: Check at least 1-2 moats above. If none apply, this company competes on price = risky. Skip.**

---

### 4. MANAGEMENT & GROWTH OUTLOOK
**Question: Will the company execute on its growth promise?**

- [ ] **Management has a track record of execution?**
  - Where to check: Past 3 annual reports → Did they deliver on stated goals?
  - Red flag: Missed guidance repeatedly = skip.

- [ ] **Clear capital allocation strategy?**
  - Examples: Dividends, buybacks, debt reduction, reinvestment in R&D
  - Red flag: "We'll decide later" or constant M&A bets = unclear strategy.

- [ ] **Promoter (founder/main shareholder) has skin in the game?**
  - Where to check: Annual report → Shareholding pattern
  - Red flag: Promoter shareholding <30% = possible misalignment.

- [ ] **Analyst consensus and internal growth forecast: ≥12% revenue CAGR next 3-5 years?**
  - Where to check: Screener reports, analyst reports (free on moneycontrol, bseindia)
  - Why: Without growth, valuation won't re-rate upward.

**DECISION: Check 3+ above. If 2+ are failures, skip.**

## FINAL CHECKLIST: Buy/Don't Buy Decision

**Pass Quality Metrics?** (Section 1)
- ✓ Yes → Continue
- ✗ No → **SKIP THIS STOCK**

**Pass Valuation Check?** (Section 2)
- ✓ Yes → Continue
- ✗ No → **WAIT FOR BETTER PRICE**

**Pass Moat Check?** (Section 3)
- ✓ 1+ moats found → Continue
- ✗ No moats → **SKIP THIS STOCK**

**Pass Management Check?** (Section 4)
- ✓ 3+ criteria met → Continue
- ✗ <3 criteria → **SKIP THIS STOCK**

---

## If You Passed All Four Sections: **YOU CAN BUY**

### Entry Allocation
- **First purchase:** Invest ₹2.5-4L (depends on total portfolio size)
- **Dollar-cost average:** Spread remaining into 2-3 additional buys over next 2-3 months
- **Position size target:** This stock should be 6-10% of total portfolio

### Position Management
- **Hold period:** Minimum 3 years (unless thesis breaks)
- **Rebalancing trigger:** If position grows >12% of portfolio, trim to 10%
- **Profit-taking:** At 100% gain, trim 20% and bank the profit
- **Stop-loss:** If thesis breaks (earnings miss, management change, competitive threat), sell 30-50%

---

## EXIT CHECKLIST: When to Sell

**Sell 30-50% immediately if ANY of these happen:**
- [ ] Company's FCF turns negative for 2+ quarters
- [ ] Management changes unexpectedly (CFO, CEO)
- [ ] Debt/Equity ratio exceeds 1.0x (was <0.5x)
- [ ] Revenue growth drops below 5% for 2 consecutive years
- [ ] Profit margins compress >300 basis points
- [ ] New competitor enters with significant market share
- [ ] Regulatory/legal threat (investigation, license cancellation)

**Sell all if:**
- Stock reaches fair value + 50% upside = thesis is "priced in"
- Holding >50% gain for 3+ years = thesis fully played out
